The global pilot shortage is accelerating. Remote and northern communities are the most affected. Traditional aviation cannot keep up. The solution must be autonomous.

660K
New pilots needed globally

Over the next 20 years, the global aviation industry will need 660,000 new pilots to meet demand.

Source: Boeing Pilot and Technician Outlook, 2025. boeing.com

17K
Global pilot shortage by 2032

The global pilot shortage is projected to reach 17,000 by 2032, with remote and northern routes impacted first and hardest.

Source: Oliver Wyman, "Industry Efforts Are Easing Pilot Shortage Severity", 2023. oliverwyman.com

117
Canadian communities, no road access

117 Canadian communities have no road access and are reachable only by plane or seasonal ferry.

Source: Institute for Research on Public Policy (IRPP), January 2024. irpp.org

Pilot shortage in Canada

Canada is projected to face a shortage of 5,000 pilots, threatening northern and remote route operations.

Source: Gitnux Pilot Shortage Statistics Report, 2026. gitnux.org

Field Performance

BUILT FOR
ANY SURFACE.

Landing gear engineered for grass, gravel, dirt, and snow, JENU goes where conventional cargo aircraft cannot.

01
GRAVEL STRIPS
Unpaved gravel runways common in northern and remote territories, JENU operates on surfaces inaccessible to conventional cargo aircraft.
02
GRASS FIELDS
Unimproved grass airstrips adjacent to farming, humanitarian, and remote industrial operations.
03
DIRT RUNWAYS
Compacted earth strips in arid and semi-arid environments where paved infrastructure does not exist.
04
SNOW & ICE
Seasonal ice runways and compacted snow strips serving arctic and sub-arctic operations year-round.
05
SHORT PAVED STRIPS
Existing regional airstrips too short for conventional turboprop cargo aircraft, fully accessible to JENU.
06
